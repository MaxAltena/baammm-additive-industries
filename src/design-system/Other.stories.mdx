# Other

## Card

In order to group items visually, cards can be used. Cards are simply rectangles with added drop shadows,
giving the impression that the content of this card belongs together and is part of the foreground of the application.

<ThemeProvider theme={theme}>
	<Card style={{ maxWidth: '500px' }}>
		<Heading type="subtitle">Machine controls</Heading>
		Lorem ipsum dolor sit amet, consectetur adipisicing elit. Ab adipisci, aliquid amet aspernatur consequuntur delectus
		eius error et explicabo fuga inventore itaque iure quasi quidem, quis quo sint temporibus veritatis.
	</Card>
</ThemeProvider>

## Button

The button is a simple but important interface element that clearly gives users an indication
that they can perform certain actions.

<ThemeProvider theme={theme}>
	<Button label="Start Print" primary={true} onClick={() => alert('Print job has started on Machine 1')} />
	&nbsp;
	<Button label="Cancel" onClick={() => alert('Print job has been canceled')} />
	&nbsp;
	<Button
		label="Turn off"
		size="small"
		primary={true}
		backgroundColor={theme.colors.status.red}
		onClick={() => alert('Machine 1 has been shut down')}
	/>
</ThemeProvider>

## Progress bar

To indicate the status of a certain process that might take a while, progress bars are used.
The progress bar gives users a clear indication of how far along a certain process has come.
It is recommended to accompany a progress bar with an indication of when the process is due to be finished.

<ThemeProvider theme={theme}>
	<ProgressBar percentage={30} />
	<br />
	<ProgressBar percentage={66} />
</ThemeProvider>
